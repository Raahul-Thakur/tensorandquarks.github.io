Supernova remnants (SNRs) ‚Äî the ghosts of exploded stars ‚Äî shine brightly in the GLEAM-X maps. Their steep synchrotron spectra make them ideal low-frequency targets.

Tiny but mighty, planetary nebulae appear as flat-spectrum dots ‚Äî the final whispers of dying Sun-like stars. GLEAM-X captures these with surprising clarity, enriching models of stellar evolution and ionisation dynamics.

GLEAM-X also picks up the faint hum of pulsars. By cross-matching with known catalogs, astronomers found dozens of detections ‚Äî and potential new candidates.
